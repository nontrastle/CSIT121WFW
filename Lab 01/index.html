A Turkey Burger that's better than a Beef Burger

First things first, let's gather the ingredients. You'll need:

  • One pack of ground turkey
  • A grren pepper
  • One egg
  • A slice of bread
  • One yellow onion
  • A quarter cup of milk of choice (if non-dairy use plain unflavored)
  • Seasonings
    • Salt
    • Pepper
    • Parsely
    • Oregano
    • Season All
  • Cast iorn skillet and oil of choice

Prep

Next it is time to prep our ingredients

  1. Rinse ground trukey under cool running water, set on a paper towel, blot dry and then place in a bowl.
  2. Dice half of the green peper into small pieces and set aside.
  3. Remove crust from the slice of bread and discard then tear the slice of bread into tiny pieces and set aside.
  4. Dice half of the yellow onion into tiny pieces and set aside.
  5. Place cast iron skillet on stove top and add a little oil. Turn fire down low as to let it warm up.

Begin

Time to combine the ingridents.

Combine Dry Ingredients
In the bowl with the ground turkey combine the diced green pepers, bread crumbs, onion and the seasoning. Season to taste. Let your heart lead you!
Add Wet Ingredients
Next crack the egg into the bowl with the ground turkey. Carefull not to get any shells. No one wants a crunchy burger.Then add the milk.
Mix it All Around
It is time to get messy! Take your hands and mix everything together until the seasonings, bread crumbs, peper and onions are all evenly distributed throughout the ground turkey. If the mixture is too dry then add a little more milk.
Shape Your Burgers
Once the ingredients have been fully mixed take a portion and begin to shape your burgers to your liking. No one will judge you if your patty is inches thick! Once the burgers are shaped set aside on a plate.
Turn up the Heat
Turn up the heat on your skillet to medium high. You want the pan hot but not burning the oil. Once the pan is hot lay that first burger down and watch the magic begin. Once the edges of the burger begin to crisp up and turn brown flip it over to let it finish cooking. Depending on how thick your burger is it ususally takes about 5min on each side.
Dress and Serve
Once you have all your burgers cooked set them a top of a paper towel and pat down to get rid of excessive oil. Take the remaing half of your onion, chop up and add to the same pan that the burgers came out of. You may need to add a little more oil to fry the onions. Once the onions are fried place atop of burgers with any other fixings of your choice. Toast burger buns or bread if you don't have buns and place burger on top and enjoy!

Resources

Hopefully you enjoyed this receipe. This is my favorite receipe becasue it is very versatile. You can use this ground turkey receipe for spaghetti, chilli, tacos, meatball subs, and the list goes on! Endless possiblites.
